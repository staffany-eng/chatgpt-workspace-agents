## What They Said

- StaffAny value should be framed around cost savings, scalability, visibility/accountability, accuracy, compliance, retention/engagement, and admin time savings.
- The core module value map is scheduling for manpower/cost control, time attendance for OT/work-hour/pay checks, and timesheets for accurate timely pay.
- ICP qualification should consider industry, country-specific headcount thresholds, organization maturity, typical outlet count, buying roles, required sales motion, and ACV.
- ICP 2 is the bread-and-butter fit, while ICP 3 needs stakeholder nurturing because sales has less direct control.
- Persona/JTBD differs by owner, HR manager, ops manager, staff, and outlet or cluster manager.
- User journeys should cover team management and payroll processing pain before claiming feature fit.
- Case-study proof patterns should connect pain to value: reduced payroll work, OT control, cross-location coordination, manpower allocation, and hardware avoidance.

## Learning Summary

- NurtureAny should use ICP stage, country headcount, outlet count, persona, and JTBD before choosing a value angle.
- Drafts and pre-demo plans should tie product features to a business value: cost, time, accuracy, visibility, compliance, trust, or flexibility.
- ICP 0 should be disqualified or moved fast; ICP 1 and ICP 2 are farmable; ICP 3 should be relationship-led and multi-stakeholder.
- Persona-specific framing matters: owners care about cost, scale, and visibility; HR cares about payroll accuracy, compliance, and less consolidation work; ops cares about scheduling, last-minute changes, and cross-outlet coordination.
- Customer examples from this deck are useful as internal proof patterns, but public name-drop use should still follow approved case-study rules.
