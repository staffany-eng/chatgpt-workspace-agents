## Evidence Extracts

- Broad values listed: save costs, scalability, accountability via visibility, accuracy, compliance, staff performance and retention via engagement, and save time on admin.
- The three module whys are scheduling for cost efficiency and adequate manpower, time attendance for OT/work-hour/pay checks, and timesheet consolidation for timely accurate pay and minimum-hour checks.
- Scheduling value examples: SPLH and target manpower save cost through optimal manpower use; shift notifications save time by communicating schedule changes immediately.
- Scheduling value examples: leave plus scheduling improves visibility by preventing shift and leave clashes; multi-outlet scheduling improves visibility across outlet staff activity.
- Scheduling value examples: collecting availability saves time by reducing WhatsApp back-and-forth.
- Time tracking value examples: OT/UT visualisation saves time and supports compliance; timeclock sidekick can reduce time theft by preventing unnecessary paid attendance hours.
- Timesheet value examples: exports save time on consolidation, shift tags categorize hours, late/absent tags give visibility, and timesheet exports improve salary accuracy and team trust.
- ICP industries listed: F&B, retail, hospitality, healthcare/clinics, and entertainment.
- ICP country thresholds: Singapore below 20 is no-fit, above 20 is possible, above 50 is good fit; Malaysia and Indonesia below 50 are no-fit, above 50 are possible, above 100 are good fit.
- ICP 0 has immature requirements and often uses spreadsheets or punch cards; ICP 1 has basic JTBD; ICP 2 starts optimization; ICP 3 covers advanced rules and long-tail enterprise-style requirements.
- ICP 2 is described as the bread-and-butter segment, with PMF achieved, brand durability, previous solution exposure, and typical SG headcount above 50 or MY/ID above 100.
- ICP 3 has large brands, multiple stakeholders, longer sales cycles, and advice to nurture relationships because sales has less direct control.
- Sales use of ICP: outbound targeting, qualification, optimal conversion rates, and ACV.
- Product use of ICP: identify JTBD and create more value for jobs that matter.
- Marketing and demand generation use ICP to qualify leads, attract the market, and give Sales quality leads and key messages.
- Owner JTBD/value: visibility, cost projection, audit trail, SPLH, labour cost projection, scale the business with cost projection, and reduce unnecessary labour-hour spend.
- HR manager JTBD/value: payroll, OT reduction, audit operations, timesheet consolidation, audit trail, timeclock sidekick, save time, and reduce error.
- Ops manager JTBD/value: manage staff across outlets, last-minute manpower adjustment, OT projection, multi-store overview, real-time updates, staff availability, mass grab, save time, reduce error, and easier communication.
- Staff JTBD/value: visibility of own timesheet, mobile clock-in/clock-out, and fewer discrepancies.
- Team-management journey has five phases: gather availability/cost inputs, assign and visualize schedules, publish schedules, handle last-minute changes, and update final schedules after operations.
- Payroll-processing journey has five phases: collect timecards, tally timeclock against actual schedule, count OT, categorize other hours, and cross-check final hours with managers and staff.
- User segments include business owners, HR, managers, and outlet managers; preferred values vary by size and role.
- Small ICP prioritizes visibility from real-time attendance and save time from flexible schedule changes.
- Mid ICP prioritizes timesheet/time savings, target-manpower/SPLH cost savings, and multi-outlet visibility.
- Big ICP prioritizes implementation ease, SPLH savings, accurate faster timesheet consolidation, and compliance with OT/work-hour/public-holiday rules.
- Kiosk Collective proof pattern: manual payroll up to 14 man-days monthly; after StaffAny, payroll man-days reduced by more than 50%, plus connected tools and accountability.
- Phoon Huat proof pattern: cloud-based scheduling and attendance supported manpower allocation during business model shifts.
- Foreword Coffee proof pattern: scheduling reduced from three hours to one hour and supported cross-location coordination.
- Petron Grandsaga proof pattern: OT control and daily justification reduced unnecessary OT, with estimated RM 500-1000 monthly savings.
- YC Enterprise proof pattern: replacing hardware-based attendance reduced maintenance and repair pain while improving real-time team visibility.
