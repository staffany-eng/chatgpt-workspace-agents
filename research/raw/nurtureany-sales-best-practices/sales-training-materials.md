## Evidence Extracts

### Rubric backbone

- The current rubric sheet defines CCC as Connect, Curiosity, Convert, and Fallback.
- Strong Connect means a short relevant opener plus a micro question in roughly 10-15 seconds.
- Curiosity should not sound like generic networking. It should use relevant peers, brands, or context to earn attention.
- Convert should present a clear yes/no next step with respectful urgency or scarcity.
- Fallback should turn rejection into a 15-minute catch-up anchored on curiosity or pain, not a dead-end close.
- The same rubric defines CBANT as Connect, Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline.
- Budget should establish value alignment and avoid positioning StaffAny as only the cheapest option.
- Authority should map the approval process and identify the person who can sign or drive the buying decision.
- Need should probe pain, operational cost, or opportunity cost rather than collect shallow interest.
- Timeline should capture contract end date, signing path, onboarding date, and decision date where relevant.

### Pre-demo preparation and I-C-BANT

- Module 2 instructor material says the three steps to win deals are pre-meeting notes and hypothesis, pre-product I-C-BANT, then product demo and negotiation.
- Pre-demo fact finding includes number of employees, industry, lead source, intent or why now, stakeholders, current stack, timeline, and contract end.
- Pre-demo research is split into secondary and primary research.
- Primary research is mandatory for opportunities above 10k ACV, with examples such as outlet visit, mystery call, multithreading, and ex-employee context.
- Hypothesis planning should cover likely interest, buying alternatives, what to show, what to avoid, and game plans A and B.
- I-C-BANT stands for Introduction, Connect, Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline.
- Module 2 warns not to rush into demo or pricing and not to interrogate prospects.

### Pre-demo nurturing

- Module 1 instructor material says pre-demo nurturing earns the right to a meeting by Curiosity, Credibility, and Context.
- The training asks reps to add value before demo through Knowledge, Network, and Support.
- Success means the prospect sees StaffAny as relevant and worth meeting, enough context has been gathered to shape the conversation, and the prospect is open to a next step.
- Failure modes include sharing product details over WhatsApp, pushing for demo too early, and outreach with no value.
- The cadence is a five-touch rhythm over about 14 to 18 days: hook, contextual follow-up, K/N/S value, re-engage, and close-loop.
- The rule is that every touchpoint must earn its place instead of chasing a reply.

### Value-driven demo

- Module 3 instructor material says a demo is not a product walkthrough; it is a value conversation.
- The prospect should visualize their operation after using StaffAny, understand quantifiable benefits, learn at least three relevant things, and agree on next steps or timeline.
- The seven demo dimensions are Control and Conversational, Consultative, Contextual, Before/After, Benefits versus Features, Product Knowledge, and Negotiation.
- A strong opening focuses the demo on three areas based on shared context.
- The training says if the customer cannot explain the value back, the demo failed.
- Reps should not show everything. They should recommend workflows, contextualize with relevant case studies, and use before/after contrast at least three times.
- Benefits should come before features at least three times.
- Product knowledge should avoid false answers and limit unsupported "I will get back to you" moments.
- Negotiation should tie back to quantified value, give-and-take, and a clear timeline.

### Post-demo follow-up

- Post-demo instructor material says follow-up is about earning a decision, not sending reminders.
- Success means the rep gets a clear yes, no, or next step; identifies the real blocker; records a loss reason if lost; and sets a re-follow-up date if needed.
- Failure means generic check-ins, desperate tone, ghosting, or losing without the real reason.
- The follow-up 3C standard is Credible, Contextual, and Consistent.
- Suggested cadence includes next follow-up date on demo day, D+1 summary email and WhatsApp, D+3 case study, D+5 K/N/S value, D+10 reference or social proof, D+14 go/no-go, and D+21 breakup call.
- The breakup call should ask for the real blocker, who or what StaffAny lost to, and when to reconnect.

### Onboarding and coaching

- The MY onboarding plan aims to identify and ramp high-performing reps within 8 weeks using values, execution, structured tests, and pass/fail checkpoints.
- Month 1 focuses on certifications and minimum targets; Month 2 expects at least 8 QOs independently; Month 3 targets independent operation; Month 4 targets full ramp.
- The training sequence covers admin and product, users and process, lead generation, appointment setting, objection handling, demo, pricing, practice, tests, and roleplays.
- Older onboarding activity-point targets are useful as context but lower authority than the current Tactical Pause activity hygiene policy.

### Market, persona, and value proposition

- The Target Market, User Journey, and Value Proposition deck maps StaffAny value to save costs, scalability, accountability through visibility, accuracy, compliance, staff performance and retention through engagement, and save time.
- The same source says "save time" alone is a weaker standalone value proposition.
- Smaller ICP accounts are more associated with visibility and save time.
- Mid-sized accounts are more associated with save time, save cost, and visibility.
- Larger accounts are more associated with save time, save cost, accuracy, and compliance.
