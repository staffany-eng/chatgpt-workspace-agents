## Evidence Extracts

- Coaching overview: Gong positions coaching as a data-driven program built around recorded calls, pipeline/deal inspection, coachable moments, progress tracking, and business outcomes. It also says AI Call Reviewer can suggest scorecard answers or score calls automatically from scorecard criteria. Source: Introduction to coaching.
- Feedback loop: Gong's coaching quick start routes users from the call page into feedback requests, comments, internal sharing, and scorecard scoring. Source: Introduction to coaching.
- AI review unit: Gong describes AI Call Reviewer as structured feedback on sales and customer conversations using scorecards. It can suggest individual scorecard answers or score entire calls automatically using predefined criteria. Source: AI Call Reviewer.
- AI review evidence base: Gong says AI Call Reviewer searches the call transcript to answer scorecard questions configured for AI. Human scorers can accept, edit, or replace suggested answers before submitting. Source: AI Call Reviewer.
- Scorecard purpose: Gong frames scorecards as structured feedback that helps managers and enablement teams focus on explicit coaching opportunities, adoption, and performance impact. Source: All about scorecards.
- Scorecard dimensions: Gong's example AE discovery and demo questions cover open-ended discovery, role understanding, pain, business impact, benefits/capabilities, ability to solve pain, buying process, concerns, next steps, preparation, agenda, value-led functionality, tailoring, and firm future commit. Source: All about scorecards.
- Scorecard rollout: Gong recommends starting with one or two scorecards, defining what good sounds like, aligning scorers on score meaning, setting scoring KPIs, then using search and team insights to learn from scored calls over time. Source: All about scorecards.
- Call page shape: Gong's call page combines call title/account/time, sharing/follow-up actions, a recording player, speaker soundtrack, call stats, comments, topics, AI answers, highlights, outline, and jump-to-moment navigation. Source: Intro to the call page.
- Interaction metrics: Gong call search includes interaction filters such as talk ratio, interactivity, patience, longest team monologue, longest customer story, host/customer question counts, and question rates. Source: Search for calls.
- Search and collaboration: Gong call search filters include participants, account, words/phrases, trackers, call title, deal stage, date, platform, recording system, call outcome, topics, scorecard name, overall score, and comments. Source: Search for calls.
- Trackers: Gong trackers identify words, phrases, or concepts in calls and emails; smart trackers identify concepts and keyword trackers identify terms. Gong suggests grounding trackers in business priorities such as pricing rollout, new methodology, stronger objection handling, competitor differentiation, pain points, promotions, and recording consent. Source: Understanding trackers.
- Topics: Gong topics are automatically identified subjects that appear on the call page, filters, team stats, and Whisper. The docs describe topics as company-specific and usable for coaching and analyzing business-critical discussions. Source: Call topics.

## Local Interpretation Notes

- Gong's public docs describe a full coaching product loop: capture call -> call page -> transcript/search/stat evidence -> scorecard review -> manager feedback -> search/team trends.
